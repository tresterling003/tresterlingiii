About

Dallas-based creative.
Self-taught.
Self-driven.

I picked up a camera and Photoshop in high school because I was a football player who couldn't afford to pay for graphics to get recruited — so I figured it out myself. That mentality carried me to Oklahoma State where I played D1 ball while continuing to sharpen my creative skills on the side. After college I got the call — signed with the Green Bay Packers as an undrafted free agent. When football was done I went all in on the creative side, joining TPN+ Creative Agency where I worked on projects for brands like Walmart, Kroger, and Danimals. Then I went back to where it all started — Sunnyvale High School — and built their entire media program from scratch. Motion graphics, hype videos, media days, scoreboard animations, a full news channel. No templates. No shortcuts. Just one person who figured it out.

Whether you're an athlete trying to get noticed, a brand that needs to look the part, or an organization that deserves better than Canva templates — I know exactly where you're coming from. I've been that athlete. I've been that program with nothing. Now I build the kind of work that makes people stop scrolling. If you're ready to level up how you look, let's get to work.

Graphic Design · Personal Work

Untitled Collage

A self-initiated collage piece built purely out of love for the format. Unlike traditional collages that feel scattered, I designed the layered textures to follow a directional flow that compliments the angle of the athlete in the photo. The deliberate face blur shifts all attention away from identity and onto the composition itself — adding mystery and making the details do the work.
